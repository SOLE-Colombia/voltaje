Buying the best computer for me

Welcome

Hi, I’m Nicolas and, like you, I’ve been learning about how to improve my internet connection. I am not an expert on the subject, but by searching the internet, tutorials and learning from trial and error I have managed to improve the quality of the internet in areas without good connectivity.

What is how to buy a computer for you?

In this solution you will be able to clarify some important things to make the decision. On the one hand, differentiate the main characteristics of a computer and a series of recommendations based on these characteristics. You can also take into account for the analysis your needs and capabilities. I hope you can feel accompanied and confident as you go through the selection of a computer.

What is it for?

Making a decision about a purchase in areas in which we are not experts can be a difficult exercise. For this reason, we have developed this solution that serves to accompany you in this process. In the end, this solution helps you to make an informed decision and not spend too much money or end up buying a computer that does not meet your requirements.

How do I know if I need it?

To know if you need, or not, to make a purchase, there are two fundamental criteria:

  • Reflect deeply and meditatively if you need it, for this ask yourself: What need is solving the purchase of a computer, can this need be solved in another way? For example, can you do the same with the cell phone you currently have, or perhaps with a tablet?
  • If you have already corroborated that the need you are considering can only be solved with access to a computer, it is time to look at other alternatives before purchasing. For example, can you borrow a computer from the school or library, can you get a donated computer from a family member or a company?

If you have exhausted the above options and have definitely decided to buy a computer, this solution is right for you.

Options to review

  • Desktop Computer

    This is a computer designed to be installed in a static location, such as a desk or table. The hardware components are usually a computer case that houses the motherboard with the respective components.

  • Notebook computer

    Also known as a laptop, this is a computer that can be moved or transported with relative ease. Laptops are small, lightweight and are specially designed so that their batteries are capable of powering them for an extended period of time (usually 4 to 5 hours). Screen sizes range from 11 to 15 inches.

  • Moment 2: Let’s make the decision

    Now that you know the basics it’s time to make a decision, for this we have developed a series of simple questions, depending on your answer we would recommend one or another hardware feature:

    Criterion 0: Set a budget

    Now it is time to define a budget, for this you can do the math of how many days of work per month it represents to buy this computer. Finally, the decision will fall on this point, this purchase can easily mean more than a month of work, so it is very important to try to be as informed as possible before deciding.

    Criterion 1: Usage habits

    Now let’s remember some of the most relevant questions when defining our usage habits:

    • Do I need the computer to be portable or do I prefer to use it in one place?
    • Am I going to use it for video games and design programs or for office work?
    • How much information will I store?
    • Do I need to use several programs at the same time?

    With these questions and your budget you can decide on a smaller list of options. choose wisely.

Don’t be fooled

It is important to remember that when you go to a store a salesperson is usually looking for the “star product”, however many times the cost of computers from 6 months or a year ago are significantly lower than the technologies of that moment. Sometimes, you can find second hand computers that have been in good hands and make a good deal. On the other hand, it only remains for me to recommend that if possible you buy a desktop computer or a laptop that allows you to upgrade its hardware.

The computer I am writing from I bought it 13 years ago, it has gone through hard drive replacement and RAM upgrade, plus a few fixes, but now it works fine and has been a great investment for the long term.

Finally, remember that now it is very easy to find pages and [people](

Another recommendation that never hurts, is to be guided by the reputation of the brand and the seller. In my experience I recommend the ASUS brand in terms of quality-price ratio, although from SOLE, we have also done well with the Lenovo brand.

How much is enough?

This question is difficult to answer, but I think we can rephrase it as follows: Does the option you chose meet the need and intention you projected at the beginning? In my case, 13 years ago I bought a computer for college, but also one that would allow me to play video games, download and watch a lot of movies. That is to say, going for the minimum (to be useful for college) I should have selected a laptop, light weight, with good battery, a small screen, an intermediate processor, 8GB of RAM and a generic graphics card. However, I did the opposite, I selected a large, heavy, low-battery computer with a dedicated graphics card, what they would say a “gamer” computer: The ASUS N53SV.

By not meditating well my decision I ended up buying a computer too big and heavy for its transport and with low battery. Finally its main function was to serve for the university, the video games and movies was something secondary. At the moment, although I use it, I hardly transport it, it ended up being in practice a desktop computer. So from SOLE Voltage, we do not want you to fall into mistakes like mine! Always remember that many times “less is more”.

With this we have just seen, you can no longer put your fingers in your mouth, it’s time to make the decision!
