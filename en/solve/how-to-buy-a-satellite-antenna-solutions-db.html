Welcome

Hi, I’m Nicolas and I’m learning about how the internet works. I’m not an expert on the subject, but through internet searches, tutorials and, above all, a lot of curiosity, I’ve learned how to set up functional internet solutions.

What is choosing an internet service for non-interconnected locations?

In this particular solution we will learn how to choose a satellite internet service for rural or remote areas that do not have Wi-fi or cable internet connection. For this wewill look at three technologies available in Colombia: Satellite dish, WiMax and Starlink.

What is it for?

This solution is useful to have more clarity about which satellite internet service you can choose if you are located in areas that do not have Wi-fi or cable internet connection.

How do I know if I need it?

Now, the quality of the internet service you can choose will depend on your possibilities and needs, as well as the availability of internet providers in your region. This solution is an option that you can pay for together with the people in the community where you live.

Options to review

  • What you need:
    • A budget.
    • A list of internet providers in your region.
    • A lot of curiosity!
    • It may help to have people in your community to review the information with, making a decision together will be easier and more profitable for everyone.

How to do it?

  • Moment 0: What are the types of internet connections that serve non-interconnected regions?

    Now let’s get down to business. Digital technologies are changing rapidly over the years. In this article we want to present you an investigation of the options available in Colombia for 2024: satellite dish, WiMax and Starlink.

    • Satellite dish

      The most popular internet option for rural or remote areas these days is the satellite dish. The great advantage of this solution is its coverage, accessibility and price, which makes it a viable and functional option. To access this technology, the company in charge must install a satellite dish in your home.

    • WiMax

      Image taken from wikipedia, https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/WiMAX#/media/Archivo:Alvarion_CPE.jpg

      The first thing you need to do is to contact a company that offers these services and has a repeater near your house to send you the internet signal. Next, you need to put an antenna on the front of your house. This antenna will have to point directly to the repeater of the company that is going to give you the service or to an intermediate connection point that is used to save the obstacles that are interposed between your antenna and its repeater. The installation of the antenna will be done by the company itself.

    • Starlink

      Image taken from Starlink, https://www.starlink.com/es/specifications

      Starlink is Elon Musk’s satellite internet project. It is a particular satellite internet that facilitates connections especially in rural areas. The company claims to be the world’s first and largest satellite constellation that uses a low Earth orbit to offer broadband, high-speed, low-latency satellite Internet, which allows you to watch streaming content, play online games, make video calls and more. To get it up and running, you must purchase an antenna kit to be placed outside your home that automatically orients itself to establish a connection in a matter of minutes, provided you have a clear view of the sky.

We already have an introduction of the available options, now we can move on to moment 1 in which we will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of each option.

  • Moment 1: Advantages and disadvantages of satellite dish connection

    Advantages

    • Global coverage: This means that people living in remote regions or rural areas can reliably access the Internet.
    • Fast access: Faster than DSL and dial-upservices with speeds of up to 25 Mbps and 100 Mbps. While satellite Internet used to have a reputation for being slow, it is now possible to enjoy speeds comparable to terrestrial connections.
    • Constant connectivity: One of the most outstanding advantages of the satellite dish is its ability to provide a constant and stable connection. Unlike terrestrial connections that can be affected by infrastructure disruptions or adverse weather conditions, satellite internet does not rely on physical cables and relies on orbiting satellites for data transmission. This guarantees a reliable and continuous connection, even in areas prone to natural disasters.
    • High flexibility and mobility: Satellite Internet also offers flexibility and mobility. You can access the Internet from any location covered by the satellite signal, making it an ideal choice for frequent travelers, remote workers or those who need connectivity in temporary or expanding areas.
    • No residential telephone service is required: It is not a requirement to have a telephone line to access this Internet service.

    Disadvantages

    • High latency: One of the main disadvantages of the satellite dish is latency, i.e. the time it takes for the signal to travel from the satellite to the Earth. Due to the distance the signal must travel, latency can be higher compared to terrestrial connections. This can affect real-time activities, such as video conferencing or online gaming, where even small delays can be noticeable.
    • High cost: Satellite internet tends to be more expensive compared to other connectivity options. Service prices are higher than other types of internet, due to expensive equipment, operating and satellite costs.
    • Weather conditions: The good performance of satellite internet may depend on good weather conditions. A clear sky is necessary for the use of the satellite dish. Signal stability may be compromised by rain or thunderstorms.
    • Data limitations: Many satellite Internet providers apply monthly data limits in their plans. This means that users have a limited amount of data they can download and upload during a given period. If this limit is exceeded, the speed may be reduced or additional charges may apply.

  • Moment 2: Advantages and disadvantages of WiMax connection

    Advantages

    • High fluidity and low latency: This allows for a fast and fluid connection with low latencies.
    • No need for a residential telephone service: It is not a requirement to have a telephone line to access the Internet service.
    • VoIP calls: There will be companies that with the installation of this technology will allow you to make VoIP calls (it is a technology that allows you to make and receive voice calls over the Internet).

    Disadvantages

    • Direct contact with the repeater is necessary: This means that the antenna you install in your house has to be oriented directly to the place from where the connection is being sent, and if possible avoiding obstacles that prevent direct vision, such as trees or buildings. This can sometimes make it necessary to install intermediate connection points to avoid obstacles.
    • Asymmetrical speeds: this means that the download speed will be much higher than the upload speed. WiMAX connections can reach speeds of up to 1Gbps at fixed points, for example a house, and 365Mbps at mobile points. Even so, it will always depend on what the company from which you contract the service offers you.
    • Congestion traffic: Speed could be affected more easily during peak hours.

  • Moment 3: Advantages and disadvantages of Starlink connection

    Advantages

    • Easy installation: The user buys a kit and can assemble it autonomously.
    • High fluency and low latency: Broadband Internet at high speed and low latency. You will have latencies of around 20ms, compared to the average of 600ms.
    • High flexibility and mobility: Starlink also offers flexibility and mobility. You can access the Internet from any location covered by the satellite signal, making it an ideal choice for frequent travelers, remote workers or those who need connectivity in temporary or expanding areas.
    • Possibility of full refund of the cost of the equipment in the first month: If you purchase the service directly with Starlink there is this possibility: ****“Try it for 30 days and, if you are not satisfied, return the equipment and get a full refund,” says Starlink on its website.

    Disadvantages

    • Weather conditions: good satellite internet performance may depend on good weather conditions. A clear sky is needed for satellite dish use. Signal stability may be compromised by rain or thunderstorms.
    • High cost: Starlink satellite internet tends to be more expensive compared to other connectivity options. This is due to the high costs associated with launching and maintaining satellites, as well as the infrastructure needed on Earth to operate the ground stations. The cost of this service is 300,000COP∗∗permonthwithaone−timeequipmentpaymentof∗∗1’900,000 COP.
    • Capacity limitations: Since the Starlink system is based on low orbiting satellites, capacity is limited and is shared among users in a given area. In congested areas, this could result in reduced speed during peak hours.
    • Interference issues: Some reports suggest that Starlink may experience interference issues, especially in densely populated areas or in regions with high satellite activity.
    • Environmental impact: The deployment of thousands of satellites in low orbit has raised concerns about environmental impact and space debris generation.
    • Need for direct view to the sky: To use Starlink, a direct view to the sky is required, which may limit its viability in areas with significant obstructions such as tall trees or buildings.

  • Moment 4: How do I choose the best option for my community?

    Now that you are clearer about each of the three options, their advantages and disadvantages, you can make a more informed decision together with your community. We consider the following to be important elements when choosing an option:

    • Availability

    Although this analysis was done in 2024 for Colombia, it may be useful for other countries. However, the first thing to do is to contact the companies that offer internet services in your region, they will be able to guide you better and tell you if they offer WiMax technology. But remember that their interest is to sell, but you are the one who decides. Satellite internet and Starlink have global coverage so you will not depend exclusively on the providers in your region.

    • Budget

    First of all, define your budget, for this you can calculate how many days of work you are willing to invest together with your community to hire this service. Don’t get into debt for an internet plan!

    Maxi Gagliano, https://www.pexels.com/photo/close-up-photo-of-money-4636069/

    • Reliability

    It is important to know if the company you selected has a good reputation or not. You can find this out from other users who have hired them and it will be vital when you have any inconvenience or have to do any kind of maintenance on the equipment.

    • Usage habits

    Depending on what your community uses the Internet for, you will be able to choose a technology that benefits you the most. Here you can evaluate if the latency is important because they need to make video calls or play online, if they are willing to hire a monthly payment service or simply as the use is occasional they only pay the internet service in the months of greater use, or if, simply, these options are not the most adequate and they prefer to continue without an internet connection.

Don’t be fooled

Define your usage and number of devices to estimate the speed and bandwidth needed, and then evaluate the technology available (satellite is a common option) and the provider’s reliability and technical support.

Research the provider’s reputation with neighbors and see if they offer specific plans for rural areas. Upload speed, download speed and latency should be adequate for your activities, and it is useful to ask about data restrictions and the quality of the provider’s infrastructure.

How much is enough?

One of these three solutions may be the right one to solve connectivity in your region, because they are all designed for remote urban locations and do not depend on a wired internet service for their operation.

You can take into account the following criteria and think about them according to the people and content you need to access:

  • For basic use (emails, social networks, browsing): A moderate download speed and a basic data plan might be sufficient.
  • For streaming and online work: A higher download speed and higher bandwidth is required to avoid interruptions.
  • For intensive upload usage (cloud, large files): It is important to look for a symmetrical connection, where the upload speed is close to the download speed.
