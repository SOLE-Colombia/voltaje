Welcome

Hi, I’m Nicolas and, like you, I’ve been learning about how to improve my internet connection. I’m not an expert on the subject, but by searching, making tutorials and learning from trial and error I have worked on improving the quality of the internet in places that don’t have good connectivity.

In this particular solution we will understand what are the criteria to choose which satellite internet best fits our connectivity needs. We will also face some ethical issues that may condition our choice.

Our advice: decide with head and heart, the option that best serves your needs and those of your community.

What is choosing satellite Internet wisely?

Satellite Internet is a technology designed so that the Internet signal is sent through a satellite located in orbit to the satellite dish of your home or business.

With satellite internet you can connect computers, cell phones, tablets, etc. This technology does not require the wiring used, for example, by fiber optic internet.

What is it for?

This solution helps you to decide which satellite internet is the most convenient to buy if you are located in a place that does not have internet connection.

In this solution we will discuss the advantages and disadvantages of three different solutions so that you can make a conscious and responsible decision: satellite dish, WiMax or Starlink.

****Before we start, we suggest that, in case you decide to buy some of the options presented in this solution, you can pay for it together with other people and thus enjoy a communal use of the Internet.

Si por el contrario respondiste no a todas las preguntas anteriores, cuentas con una mala conexión a internet y ya revisaste otras soluciones, te invitamos a contemplar la compra de internet a traves de tres opciones: Internet satelital, WiMax o Starlink.

Before we start, some ethical questions

Do you think that everyone, regardless of where they are, should have access to the Internet? In Colombia, for example, a Legislative Act of 2021 established and elevated the internet as a fundamental right, which makes it an obligation to extend internet coverage to communities and territories that are not connected. On the other hand, international organizations such as the UN affirm that denying access to the Internet is like denying the right to freedom of expression.

This may sound great, but the reality is complex and unequal. On the one hand, not all people and communities have internet: about one third of the world’s population remains offline, some 2.6 billion people. It is not in the interest of Internet companies to reach places where there is no infrastructure, as the chances of profitability are lower. This widens and maintains inequality in terms of Internet access. On the other hand, the deployment of thousands of satellites in low orbit has generated concerns about the environmental impact and the generation of space debris.

So, at SOLE Colombia, we see satellite internet as one of the existing options, especially designed for places that have no internet connection, nor nearby telecommunication towers to distribute it. If this is not your case, maybe you can find a more conscious and adjusted solution to your needs in the content of this guide.

How do I know if I need this solution?

Before we start, we would like to ask you some questions: Do you currently have regular or good quality fiber optic or mobile internet? Where you live, can you see telecommunication towers that distribute internet, are there companies that can offer you internet service at a good cost? If you answered yes to any of these questions, we recommend you to continue with your mobile or fiber optic internet and look for other solutions in this guide that will allow you to improve your internet connection. But don’t go away! We invite you to continue reading this guide to learn more about satellite internet and make your own decisions.

If you answered no to all of the above questions, have a bad internet connection and have already reviewed other solutions, we invite you to consider buying internet through three options: Satellite Internet, WiMax or Starlink.

Options to review

In this solution we will work with three technologies available in Colombia: Satellite Internet, WiMax and Starlink for the year 2024. The estimated reading time of this solution is about 15 minutes, but do not panic, you can read these recommendations with the help of other people. Remember that in SOLE Voltage we want to promote the collective use of the Internet.

  • Satellite Internet

    The most popular rural internet option is satellite internet. Although the name is self-explanatory, it is the connection to the internet via satellites. The great advantage of this option is that it can reach anywhere, including the most remote rural areas! To access this technology the company will need to install a satellite dish in your home.

  • WiMax

    Imagen tomada de wikipedia, https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/WiMAX#/media/Archivo:Alvarion_CPE.jpg

    El funcionamiento de WiMAX es sencillo, ya que es un repetidor de la empresa que quieres contratar emitiendo las señales de microondas que te llegan a la antena. Por lo tanto, lo primero que necesitas es contactar con una empresa que ofrezca estos servicios y que tenga un repetidor cerca de tu casa para poder enviarte la señal. Luego, tienes que poner una antena en la fachada de tu casa. Esta antena tendrá que apuntar directamente al repetidor de la empresa que te va a dar el servicio o a un punto de conexión intermedio que se utilice para salvar los obstáculos que se interpongan entre tu antena y su repetidor. La instalación de la antena la hará la propia compañía.

  • Starlink

    Image taken from Starlink, https://www.starlink.com/es/specifications

    Starlink is Elon Musk’s satellite internet project. It is a particular satellite internet that facilitates connections especially in rural areas. The company claims to be the world’s first and largest satellite constellation that uses a low Earth orbit to offer broadband, high-speed, low-latency satellite Internet. This allows you to watch streaming content, play online games, make video calls and more. To get it up and running, the user buys a kit with everything needed, including the antenna to be placed outdoors, which automatically orients itself to establish a connection in a matter of minutes, as long as the sky is clear.

We already have an introduction of the available options, now we want you to know the criteria you should take into account to choose the option that suits you best.

What should I take into account to make this decision?

Availability: Are these options available for the place where I live?

Although this analysis was done in 2024 for Colombia, it may be useful for other countries. To know what is the availability of these three options you must take into account that, unlike WiMax, satellite Internet and Starlink have global coverage, so they will not depend exclusively on the providers in your region. For the WiMax option, the first thing you should do is to contact the companies that offer internet services in your region, they will be able to guide you and tell you if they offer this technology.

Weather conditions: Can the weather affect the internet signal?

  • In the case of Starlink and satellite Internet , the quality of the Internet will depend on weather conditions. For the use of the satellite dish a clear sky is needed, for this reason the stability of the signal could be compromised by rain or thunderstorms.
  • In the case of WiMax, keep in mind that a direct contact with the repeater is necessary, this means that the antenna you install in your house has to be oriented directly to the place where the connection is being sent, and if possible avoiding obstacles that prevent direct vision, such as trees or buildings. Sometimes it may be necessary to install intermediate connection points to avoid obstacles.

Signal and coverage: Which option offers the best quality?

  • The advantage ofsatellite Internet is that it has global coverage, which means that people living in remote regions or rural areas can reliably access the Internet. One of the most outstanding advantages of satellite internet is its ability to provide a constant and stable connection. Unlike terrestrial connections that can be affected by interruptions in the infrastructure or adverse weather conditions. Satellite internet does not rely on cables. This guarantees a reliable and continuous connection, even in areas prone to natural disasters.

  • However, the disadvantage of satellite internet is that many providers offer a limited amount of data that can be downloaded and uploaded during a given period. If this limit is exceeded, the speed may be reduced or additional charges may apply.

  • In the case of WiMax, the advantage is that no home phone service is required. It is not a requirement to have a telephone line to access the Internet service.

  • However, a disadvantage is that the download Internet speed will be much higher than the upload speed. WiMAX connections can reach speeds of up to 1Gbps at fixed points, for example a home, and 365Mbps at mobile points. Even so, it will always depend on what the company from which you contract the service offers you. Keep in mind that the speed could be affected more easily during peak hours.

  • For its part, Starlink offers an easy installation that you and your community can carry out: the company offers the user to buy a kit with the necessary instructions to assemble it. Starlink also offers flexibility and mobility. You can access the Internet from any location covered by the satellite signal, making it an ideal choice for frequent travelers, remote workers, or those who need connectivity in temporary or expanding areas.

  • However, since the Starlink system relies on low orbiting satellites, capacity is limited and shared among users in a given area. In congested areas, this could result in reduced speed during peak hours, although its coverage is improving all the time. Some reports suggest that Starlink may experience interference issues, especially in densely populated areas or in regions with high satellite activity. For this reason, keep in mind that using Starlink requires a direct view of the sky, which may limit its viability in areas with significant obstructions such as tall trees or buildings.

What signal speed do I need?

By latency we mean the time it takes for the signal to travel from the satellite to Earth. In the case of Starlink , broadband Internet is high speed and low latency. It will have latencies of around 20ms, compared to the average of 600ms.

On the other hand, WiMax allows a fast and fluid connection with low latencies.

However, the speed of satellite internet can be slower compared to terrestrial connections. This can affect real-time activities, such as video conferencing or online gaming, where even small delays can be noticeable.
