What is building a homemade 3G antenna?

In this particular solution we will build an antenna with easily accessible materials that will allow to increase the 3G and 4G signal strength of a cell phone or a MiFi modem.

Below, you will find an instruction manual that tries to be as precise and detailed as possible so that you can feel accompanied and safe as you progress in this hack.

The estimated construction time is about 6 to 7 hours and consists of 8 moments, but do not panic, you can make the antenna a few at a time and with the help of other people, remember that in SOLE Voltage we want to promote the collective use of the internet. We also made a video with the same steps in case it makes it easier for you to follow the instruction by this means.

What is it for?

In this recipe, we are going to build a homemade antenna prepared to receive waves that carry the 4G signal - those of mobile Internet - in order to improve the quality of the connection from your phone, or a shared modem(MiFi).

What do you need?

  • Materials
    1. 3 mm thick aluminum rod (it has to be exactly 3 mm).
    2. 50 cm of plastic conduit.
    3. Fast drying epoxy glue.
    4. Good wave.
    5. 10 mt of coaxial cable.
    6. 2 presses or clamps.
    7. 2 pressure clamps.
    8. 20 cm of copper wire.
    9. A ruler (even better if you have a caliper, it allows you to be more accurate).
    10. Reamer (If you get an electric polisher with a fine cutting disc, you will save a lot of time).
    11. High precision.
    12. Fine point indelible marker.
    13. Pencil.
    14. Soldering iron, tin, flux.
    15. Tweezers and pliers (the latter is optional).
    16. Metallic file.
    17. Work table with good lighting.
    18. Plastic ties.
    19. Pole to mount the antenna (a bamboo as long as possible or a rigid rod of any material).
    20. Scalpel, cutter.
    21. Wire stripper (optional).
    22. Cell phone or shared modem (MiFi).

How to do it?

  • Step 0: Before starting, some recommendations for your care.

    This solution has an intermediate level of difficulty and involves the use of some tools that can cause accidents if you are not careful. For this reason it is important to take into account the following recommendations:

    • When using any cutting element place it in such a way that the cutting force is directed away from your body and not towards it.
    • Keep pets or children away from the work area, mainly because of the easy contact with a hot soldering iron.
    • Before cutting, make sure that the presses, clamps or clamps that hold the material do not allow the slightest movement of the material when applying any type of force.
    • When using solder and epoxy glue, be especially careful not to damage the work surface.
    • Do not use the antenna during thunderstorms since the antenna can be a lightning rod that attracts lightning to your environment.

  • Step 1: Check the materials

    Before starting, check and have at hand all the necessary elements to work with. In case you don‚Äôt know any of the materials you can check the photo showing the materials listed. Purchases can be made at the nearest hardware store, the materials are common, so it should not be a problem to find them. Perhaps, the most important item on the list is the aluminum rod and it may be a little harder to get, sometimes you can find items made with this type of rod in the metal brackets they sell in stores that bring many Chinese products. At SOLE Voltage we have used different metals: copper, welding rods and galvanized wire. We opted to buy a 3mm steel welding rod. If you can‚Äôt find aluminum in the hardware store, you can ask for other options, the important thing is that its diameter is as close as possible to 3mm or 1/8 of an inch.

    Now organize the workspace, have these instructions at hand and divide the tasks among the participants of the process: someone can be in charge of measuring, others of cutting, ordering and gluing.

  • Step 2: Understand the schematic

    Here is the final schematic and some of the parts of the assembly to give you a better understanding of the final assembly.

  • Step 3: Measuring, cutting and marking

    It‚Äôs time to get started! Here we get down to work, you must cut each of the 10 elements that make up the antenna. With the help of the caliper (or ruler), you have to mark the length of each one and cut them with the saw or the polishing machine. It is recommended that you cut one or half a millimeter extra and finish adjusting their length by filing the edges with the metal file. Below is a list of the length of the 10 metal rods that you must cut, in the world of antennas these have their specific names (director, reflector and dipole) related to the function they serve.

    This is the list of cuts that are necessary:

    1. Reflector: 8.0 cm

    2. Dipole: 18 cm (This should be the length of the element, the bent rod should be exactly 7.6 cm, for now just cut the rod to this length).

    3. Director #1: 7.6 cm

    4. Director #2: 7.5 cm

    5. Director #3: 7.4 cm

    6. Director #4: 7.4 cm

    7. Director #5: 7.3 cm

    8. Director #6: 7.3 cm

    9. Director #7: 7.2 cm

    10. Director #8: 7.2 cm

  • Step 4: Bend the dipole

    The dipole is an element bent in the shape of a ‚ÄúC‚Äù. Its overall length should be 7.6 cm, but the rod should be a little longer (about 18.0 cm). Making this piece is a trial and error task. In my case I have found it helpful to slowly bend the rod against a 2 cm diameter circular tube to give it the curvature.

  • Step 5: Assembling the antenna

    We are already half way through the process. Don‚Äôt be discouraged! ****Now it‚Äôs time to assemble the elements on the channel. This can be replaced by wood or any other material at hand, as long as it does not conduct electricity.

    On the surface of the trough, the following distances must be marked, over which each of the rod cuts made in the previous step will be placed.

    1. Reflector: 1.0 cm
    2. Dipole: 5.0 cm
    3. Director #1: 6.2 cm
    4. Director #2: 9.2 cm
    5. Director #3: 12.8 cm
    6. Director #4: 17.0 cm
    7. Director #5: 21.7 cm
    8. Director #6: 26.7 cm
    9. Director #7: 31.9 cm
    10. Director #8: 37.4 cm

    Once you have marked the lines you can glue each of the rods with the epoxy glue on the trough, however, we have found that this configuration is very fragile and makes our antenna not last very long. For this reason we found a better way to attach the rods to the trough. For this we can use the hot soldering iron that with its fine tip can easily go through the plastic, then with this tool we will pierce the sides until it has a sufficient diameter to pass the rod. Take advantage of as soon as the plastic is hot just pierce it to pass the rod and make sure it has the right diameter. This should be done on both sides of the gutter so that the rod can pass through from side to side.

    Once you have finished drilling the 20 holes, you must disassemble the trough and use the metal file to remove the burrs from the plastic to allow the rods to enter easily.

    Now, before positioning each of the rods we must make a small cut to facilitate the entry of the dipole, for this we must disassemble the channel and with the saw to make a vertical cut so that it crosses the sides of the second measure (5.0 cm) to reach the hole that we opened with the soldering iron.

    Once the dipole is embedded we can insert the other rods according to the order already mentioned.

    s

    A little epoxy glue can be added on the rod to ensure that the elements are well adhered to the trough. Wait the necessary time for the glue to dry before continuing to attach the wire to your new antenna.

  • Step 6: Prepare the cable

    While the glue is drying, you can proceed with the preparation of the

    coaxial cable

    (you can review the previous post to understand how a coaxial cable is formed before disassembling it).

    To do this part you can use a wire stripper or a cutter to separate each layer of the cable, you must be careful in the cut to avoid breaking the first layer which is a wire mesh. Thus, at one end you have to leave free the copper wire and the cable mesh. You can carefully separate the mesh and then braid it again.

    At the other end, with the copper wire, make a wave, which will be the element that comes into contact with the phone, or Mifi, to improve the signal.

  • Step 7: Solder the dipole

    This is one of the most careful steps, after this we move on to the assembly. Remember that the dipole is one of the fundamental components of the antenna, this must be soldered to the end of the coaxial cable that we separated in the mesh and the copper wire, and this in turn is connected with a copper wire bridging each end of the dipole. First we make the bridge with the copper wire as shown in the image, where each end is braided and then soldered.

    Once we have the copper wire soldered to the two ends of the coaxial we can attach it to the dipole, for this we can first tighten it a little with the pliers and then proceed to solder.

    Remember that the dipole must be perpendicular to the trough, so to avoid sudden movements of the cable that change this position we must secure the cable with plastic ties.

  • Step 8: Final assembly

    Finally we are done, now you can assemble and install the antenna! To do this, make sure it is well adjusted with plastic ties at the top of the pole, the antenna should be oriented so that the dipole is as close as possible to the pole.

    Remember also to tie the cable to the pole, so that it does not come loose from the welding points on the antenna. Now, raise the pole and make sure that the position of the antenna is facing the direction of the nearest cell tower.

    Now, on the other end of the cable, place your cell phone so that the copper coil is touching the back of the device.

    The antenna addressing process must be done slowly and carefully, the waves are very sensitive to movement. First, they can establish the 0 position and, from there, they can be rotated to the right or left in small intervals. After each movement, you can check the quality of connection to the cellular network from the ‚ÄúSettings‚Äù screen of the phone. This is a number expressed in decibels (Db), the higher it is, the lower the signal quality. You can also do this by looking at the strength of the signal icon on the top of the cell phone. The challenge is to find the place where the number of decibels is lower or where the signal strength icon is higher.

    Now, you can proceed to secure the pole in this position and that‚Äôs it, let‚Äôs go sailing!

Recommendations for points of possible difficulty

If you are using the solder and it keeps slipping, it must be that the tin solder you possibly bought is not compatible with the metal of the rods you are using, so we recommend you to look for another solder or try to make contact between the wire and the rod by tightening them well by means of a wire. Normally each metal is related to a solder of the same metal (steel to steel solder, tin to tin solder).

How do we know if this solution works?

We can know if this solution works by doing speed tests, searching with these words some page on the Internet, this will give you as a result the amount of Megabits per second (Mb/s) both in downloading and uploading information. On the other hand you can measure the quality of the connection to the network using decibels (Db), this is explained in the ‚ÄúFinal Assembly‚Äù section above. To be able to contrast you can make measurements with and without the antenna to recognize how much the signal improves with this device.

Why would this solution work?

This solution can work to improve the connectivity conditions temporarily. This is because the antenna will serve as an amplifier of the emission and reception wave of your devices.
